Research Focus

Our research focuses on developing efficient, adaptable, and scalable AI systems through innovative co-design approaches across three key areas:

Efficient AI Algorithms

We develop AI algorithms that optimize computational efficiency while maintaining high accuracy, much like how a sponge can squeeze out redundancy. Recent exemplary work includes MixRT (3DV’24) and Fragment-Pruning (NeurIPS’24).

Adaptive AI Hardware Architectures

We design hardware architectures that dynamically adapt to diverse AI workloads and deployment scenarios, similar to how a sponge can mold itself into different forms. Recent exemplary work includes Uni-Render (HPCA’25) and Fusion3D (MICRO’24, Best Paper Award).

Scalable AI Infrastructure and Systems

We build scalable systems and infrastructure that enable efficient training and deployment of AI applications across scales, from edge devices to large-scale data centers, much like how a sponge can expand to absorb more. Recent exemplary work includes Scale-GNN (DAC’25) and HW-NAS-Bench (ICLR’21, Spotlight).
